Transitional Dressing: 9 Pieces to Take You From Summer to Fall 2026

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

There's an awkward stretch of the year that nobody dresses well for. It's too warm for a coat, too cool for the strappy sundress you've been living in — and the shops are suddenly full of wool while you're still in sandals.

The answer isn't a whole new wardrobe. It's a handful of pieces that bridge the gap — items you can wear bare-armed in August heat and layer up when September turns. Buy these now and you get two seasons of wear from a single purchase, which is the only kind of shopping that makes sense at this time of year.

Here are nine of them, and how to wear each one both ways.

1. The midi shirt dress

If you buy one transitional piece, make it this. A midi shirt dress works alone with sandals when it's 30°C, and works just as hard over a fine-knit long-sleeve top with boots when it isn't. The buttons are the trick — open it fully and it becomes a light layer over jeans and a tee, which is a genuinely different outfit from the same hanger.

Wear it now: bare legs, flat sandals, sleeves rolled.

Wear it later: thin knit underneath, tights, ankle boots, belt at the waist.

2. A leather crossbody in a warm mid-tone

Straw bags have a hard stop in September. A structured leather crossbody doesn't — it's the bag that carries you through the transition and out the other side. Go for tan, cognac or chocolate. Black is versatile but reads a little severe against summer colours; a warm brown bridges both palettes without effort.

Wear it now: against linen and white cotton, where warm leather stops the outfit looking washed out.

Wear it later: with charcoal, camel and cream — it's already the right temperature.

3. White trainers (and the sock trick)

The rule that you can't wear white after summer was never real, and it certainly isn't real for footwear. A clean, low-profile trainer is the single most transitional shoe there is. The upgrade for autumn isn't a different shoe — it's a different sock. Bare ankles now, a visible ribbed sock later, and the same trainer reads completely differently.

Wear it now: cropped trousers, no socks, linen shirt.

Wear it later: straight-leg denim, chunky sock, oversized knit.

4. The oversized linen-blend shirt

Not a linen shirt — a linen blend. Pure linen creases into oblivion and stops feeling right the moment the temperature drops; a blend keeps the texture and loses the fragility. Size up. The whole point is that it functions as a shirt in August and a light jacket in October, thrown over a t-shirt with the sleeves pushed up.

Wear it now: buttoned, tucked, with shorts or a slip skirt.

Wear it later: open, over everything — the layer between "no jacket" and "actual coat."

5. A slip skirt in an autumn-friendly colour

Satin slip skirts are a summer staple, but the ones that survive into fall are the ones you buy in the right colour. Skip pastels; go for olive, rust, chocolate, deep burgundy or charcoal. Same skirt, same drape — but a burgundy slip skirt with a chunky knit and boots is an autumn outfit, while a lilac one is stranded in July.

Wear it now: fitted tank, sandals, minimal jewellery.

Wear it later: oversized knit tucked at the front, tall boots, longline coat.

6. A watch on a leather strap

Metal bracelet watches feel summery — bright, cool-toned, worn against a tan. A watch on a leather strap is the version that carries into autumn without missing a beat. It's also the easiest way to make a very simple outfit — jeans, white tee, trainers — look considered rather than accidental.

Wear it now: against bare wrists and rolled sleeves.

Wear it later: peeking out from under a knit cuff — arguably where it looks best.

7. Fine chains you can layer

The jewellery that transitions best is the jewellery you can add to. A single fine chain sits nicely against an open collar in summer; come autumn, layer two or three at different lengths and they read as intentional styling rather than a leftover summer habit. Mixing metals is entirely fine now — a gold chain and a silver one together looks more modern than a matched set.

Wear it now: one chain, open shirt, bare neck.

Wear it later: three chains at graduated lengths over a fine knit or roll-neck.

8. Straight-leg denim in a mid wash

Not skinny, not ultra-baggy — straight. It's the cut that works with sandals now and boots later, and a mid wash sits comfortably against both summer brights and autumn earth tones. Length matters more than anything: a hem that grazes the top of the foot works barefoot in sandals and sits correctly over an ankle boot.

Wear it now: rolled once, flat sandals, linen shirt.

Wear it later: over ankle boots, oversized knit, crossbody bag.

9. A lightweight scarf you'll actually use twice

The one people skip. A large, lightweight scarf in cotton or modal is a genuine two-season buy — worn loose and open over bare shoulders on a cool evening now, and wrapped properly once the mornings turn. Buy it in a colour that connects your summer and autumn wardrobes: rust, olive, camel, or a print that carries both.

Wear it now: loose over a sleeveless dress on a cool evening.

Wear it later: wrapped, over a jacket, as the finishing layer.

The two rules that matter

If you take one thing from this list, make it the principle rather than the pieces.

  • Buy for the layer, not the season. A piece earns its place if you can picture it working two ways — one with skin showing, one with something over the top. If you can only picture it one way, it's a one-season buy, and at this point in the year that's poor value.
  • Shift the palette before you shift the wardrobe. You don't need new clothes to look seasonally right in September. You need the same clothes in rust, olive, chocolate and cream rather than lemon, sky and lilac. Colour does most of the work people think fabric is doing.
The best transitional piece isn't the one that suits the weather — it's the one that suits both.
