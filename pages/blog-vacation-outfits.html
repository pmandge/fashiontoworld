Vacation Outfits That Ship Worldwide

Updated 2026 · 7 min read

The trip is booked. Now comes the part that quietly ruins the week before you leave: working out what to actually pack. Vacation dressing has a reputation for being easy — it isn't. You're packing for heat, for evenings that turn cool, for a dinner you didn't plan for, and all of it has to fit in a bag. Here's how to build a vacation wardrobe that genuinely works, and where to buy it from stores that ship wherever you are.

Plan Outfits, Not Items

The single biggest packing mistake is throwing in individual pieces you like and hoping they combine. They usually don't, and you end up wearing the same two things.

Instead, plan outfits. Count the days, count the occasions — beach days, dinners, one nicer evening, travel days — and build a complete look for each. Then look for overlap: a shirt that works over swimwear by day and with linen trousers at night is doing double duty. A dress that goes from beach to bar with a change of shoes has earned its place.

Aim for a small set of pieces that mix freely: everything should work with everything else. That's how you pack light without feeling like you're rationing.

Dress for the Destination

"Vacation" covers wildly different realities. Pack for the one you're actually going to.

Beach and resort

Lightweight, breathable, forgiving. Linen and cotton over synthetics, loose silhouettes over anything clinging. A good cover-up is the hardest-working item you'll bring — it takes you from sand to lunch without a change. Bring at least one thing you feel genuinely good in for evenings.

City break

Comfort is the whole game, because you'll walk further than you think. Breathable fabrics, layers you can shed, and shoes you've already broken in. Cities also skew slightly smarter than beaches — a dress or a shirt that reads "dinner" without needing a change is worth its space.

Somewhere that gets cold at night

Deserts, mountains, and a lot of coastlines are hot at noon and genuinely cold after dark. One warm layer you can throw over everything solves the entire problem, and it's the thing people most often forget.

Shoes: Bring Fewer Than You Want To

Shoes eat your luggage. Three pairs is almost always enough:

  • One you can walk miles in — trainers or a comfortable flat sandal, already worn in. Never travel with new shoes.
  • One for water and sand — a slide or flip-flop that you don't mind getting wet or dusty.
  • One for the evening — a block heel or a smart flat. It should go with everything else you packed, so buy it in a neutral.

The Accessories That Actually Matter

Vacation is one of the few times accessories are genuinely functional rather than decorative.

  • Sunglasses — the one thing you'll regret forgetting within an hour of landing. See accessories.
  • A hat — packable, and it does more for you than most of what's in your case.
  • A crossbody bag — hands free, belongings in front of you, works with every outfit. Browse bags.
  • Minimal jewellery — take pieces you'd be relaxed about losing, not the ones you'd mourn. Jewellery here.

Buying Resort Wear From Anywhere

Resort wear has an awkward habit of being seasonal in one hemisphere and unavailable in the other — which is precisely the problem if you're heading somewhere warm in the middle of your own winter. Shopping across worldwide-shipping stores sidesteps that entirely: someone, somewhere, is in season.

Two practical points before you order:

  • Order early. International delivery can take days or a couple of weeks. Ordering the week before you fly is how you end up buying replacements at the airport.
  • Check the returns window. If it doesn't fit and you're leaving in four days, you need to know your options before you buy, not after.
The best vacation wardrobe is the one you stop thinking about the moment you land.

Pack outfits, not items. Bring fewer shoes than you want to. Buy early enough that it actually arrives. Everything else is just holiday.
